Top 10 Skincare Myths Debunked

It's easy to get lost in all the mainstream buzz, here are the Top 10 Myths Debunked!

...

Hot water is good for your skin.

False - Hot water removes oils and natural moisture from skin which can cause dryness and itchyness. Avoid using hot water directly on skin, instead opt for warm water on face if you enjoy taking hot showers.

Read more

Lack of sleep leads to under-eye circles.

False - Genetics plays a larger role contributing to dark circles than amount of sleep, however, studies find that poor sleepers are less satisfied with their appearance.

Read more

Indoor tanning is safer than being in the sun.

False - Indoor tanning beds expose you to UV rays just as the sun does and in even more intense amounts than the sun.

You need to exfoliate your skin.

False - Your skin naturally sheds keratonocytes once a month, extra abrasion can cause irritation and enhance ongoing skin conditions.

There’s no need for sunscreen in the fall or winter.

False - UV rays are still present in the fall and winter, and though they are not as strong, you should plan on wearing sunscreen every day if you plan on being outdoors..

Read more

People with oily skin do not need to moisturize.

False - When you don’t moisturize the skin’s natural response is to create more oil. People with oily skin still need to moisturize and should look for a non-comedogenic moisturizer that will not clog pores.

Read more

You get what you pay for.

False - More expensive products do not necessarily work better than affordable options, it depends on formulation. Learn about active ingredients that are best for your skincare routine and read labels.

Read more

Your pores open and close.

False - Pores do not drasticastically open and close like “shutters” but they do become stretched out with age, temperature, and genetics.

Read more

Drinking water keeps your skin hydrated.

False - Drinking water can be beneficial for various health conditions but it does not automatically get absorbed by skin. The best method to hydrate skin would be to use a gentle cleanser and using moisturizer or hyaluronic acid to seal moisture in the skin barrier.

Read more

Only teenagers get acne.

False - Puberty triggers acne in teens, however, the same conditions can continue into adulthood due to other factors such as changes in hormones or other health conditions.
